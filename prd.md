## 1. Business Understanding (Core Identity)

**How did it start?**
TYM Electricals & Hardware started as a small shop serving the local community's basic electrical and hardware needs. Over time, consistent quality, genuine products, and reliable service built a loyal customer base. The business grew through word-of-mouth trust and a commitment to never compromise on product authenticity.

**What makes it different from other shops nearby?**
Most nearby shops focus either on price-based selling or limited product ranges. TYM stands apart by offering:
- 100% genuine branded products with proper billing and warranty
- Technical knowledge to guide customers on the right product for their need
- Wide in-stock availability reducing customer wait times
- Competitive pricing without compromising quality
- Reliable post-sale support and guidance

**Most important values:**
- Trust (genuine products, transparent pricing)
- Quality (only authentic branded inventory)
- Availability (core items always in stock)
- Service (customer-first approach)
- Integrity (fair dealing, proper billing)

**Known for in the area:**
- Electrical wiring cables (PVC, flexible)
- Switches, switchboards, and modular electrical accessories
- MCBs, distribution boards, and safety devices
- PVC pipes and plumbing fittings
- Contractor-grade tools and hardware supplies
- Industrial electrical supplies

**Kind of customers who trust most:**
- Local electricians and contractors (highest trust relationship)
- Homeowners doing renovation or repairs
- Small industry and workshop owners
- Property maintenance teams
- Builders and renovation contractors

## 2. Customer Understanding

**Regular customers:**
- Neighborhood electricians who visit almost weekly
- Homeowners in nearby localities
- Small contractors handling residential projects
- Workshop mechanics and small factory maintenance teams
- Property managers of local apartment complexes

**Problems customers usually come with:**
- Electrical faults at home or worksite
- Need for urgent replacement of switches, MCBs, wires
- Renovation projects requiring bulk materials
- Plumbing leaks and fitting replacements
- Tool breakage during active work
- Not knowing the right specification product for their need

**Most frequent questions when visiting:**
- "Is this product genuine or duplicate?"
- "What is the price of this item?"
- "Do you have this in stock right now?"
- "Can you deliver this to my site?"
- "What brand do you recommend for this?"
- "What is the warranty on this product?"
- "Can you give a better price if I buy in bulk?"

**Preference — low price, high quality, or fast availability:**
Customers want a balance. They do not want the cheapest product if it compromises quality. Most prefer **genuine quality at a fair price with immediate availability**. Reliability matters more than the lowest price for repeat customers.

**Customers to increase in future:**
- More contractors handling medium to large projects
- Industrial clients needing regular supply
- New homeowners in developing areas near the shop
- Wholesale buyers looking for bulk rates
- Commercial property maintenance teams

## 3. Current Business Process

**How customers find the shop today:**
- Word of mouth (primary source)
- Physical shop signboard and location visibility
- Google Maps listing (limited presence)
- Referrals from electricians and contractors (strongest channel)

**How they ask for products:**
- In-person visit (majority)
- Phone call (during shop hours)
- WhatsApp messages (minimal, growing)
- Friends/relatives enquiring on their behalf

**Challenges in managing customer enquiries:**
- Phone rings during peak walk-in hours causing divided attention
- No systematic way to share product images, prices, or specs remotely
- Repeatedly answering same questions (price, availability) throughout the day
- No record of enquiries made over phone or WhatsApp
- Hard to follow up on potential orders after customer hangs up

**Do customers leave due to slow responses?**
Yes. During rush hours, if phone calls go unanswered or responses are delayed, some customers reach out to other shops. Quick response directly impacts sales.

**How product availability is currently shown:**
- Physically displayed in the shop (customers see and touch)
- Verbally communicated over phone or in-person
- No digital catalog or online visibility

## 4. Product & Inventory Thinking

**Types of products sold most:**
- Electrical wires: PVC house wires, flexible wires, submersible cables
- Switches and accessories: modular switches, switchboards, holders
- Safety devices: MCBs, RCCBs, distribution boards, changeover switches
- PVC pipes and fittings: plumbing, electrical conduit
- Hardware tools: pliers, screwdrivers, testers, drills
- Fasteners: screws, nuts, bolts, anchors, clamps
- Lighting: LED bulbs, tube lights, battens, floodlights
- Fans, exhaust fans, and ventilation products

**Stock strategy:**
Core high-demand products kept always in stock. Special or slow-moving items sourced on demand. Inventory managed through manual tracking.

**How new arrivals are communicated:**
- Word of mouth to regular customers
- Physical display in the shop
- Casual mention during customer conversations

**Branded products vs local items:**
Both. Branded products from trusted manufacturers (Havells, Polycab, Finolex, Anchor, Legrand, Orient) for quality-conscious customers. Local/general items for price-sensitive buyers. Branded products form the trust backbone of the business.

**Highest profit products:**
- Wiring accessories and switchgear (MCBs, distribution boards)
- Hardware tools (good margin, repeat purchase)
- Local brand pipes and fittings
- Fasteners and small hardware items (high volume)
- Lighting products (volume-driven)

## 5. Customer Communication & Trust

**How customers contact before buying:**
- Walk into the shop to see products physically
- Call to check price and availability
- Send message on WhatsApp asking for product images and rates
- Ask through known electricians or contractors

**What builds trust before purchase:**
- Physical presence of the shop (real business, not fly-by-night)
- Seeing genuine products with proper packaging and branding
- Transparent pricing with proper bill
- Years of operation in the same location
- Recommendations from known electricians
- Owner's technical knowledge and honest recommendations

**Do customers ask for price comparison?**
Yes, frequently. Many customers call or visit multiple shops to compare rates. They return when they find our pricing fair and products genuine.

**Repeat customers and why they come back:**
Most customers are repeat. They come back because:
- Products always genuine (no fear of fake goods)
- Fair and consistent pricing
- Most items available when needed (no waiting)
- Owner gives honest advice (won't oversell)
- Good relationship and familiarity

**What makes a customer choose this shop over others:**
Trust, availability, and honest guidance. Customers value knowing they will get the right product at a fair price without being misled.

## 6. Delivery & Service Expectations

**Delivery service:**
Yes, within local area and nearby construction sites. Primarily for bulk or regular contractor orders.

**Expected speed:**
Same-day delivery is expected for urgent orders. Within a few hours for most requests. Contractors often need materials at the worksite by the time they arrive in the morning.

**Emergency or same-day delivery:**
Yes. Electricians and contractors often call when they run short on site. Same-day delivery during working hours is a common request.

**Installation or post-sale support:**
Basic technical guidance provided (which product for which use). No formal installation service, but recommendations for trusted electricians are given.

**Biggest challenge in delivery/service:**
Managing delivery requests during peak business hours with limited staff. Balancing shop floor service with outbound deliveries.

## 7. Business Growth Vision

**If successful in 1-2 years, what will change:**
- Digital presence will bring steady enquiries without relying only on walk-ins
- Better organized inventory and pricing management
- More contractor and wholesale accounts
- Ability to stock wider product range
- More efficient operations with less manual effort

**Expansion plan:**
Strengthen the current shop as the primary hub first. If demand grows, consider a second branch in a nearby developing area. Focus on depth before breadth.

**Retail or wholesale or both:**
Both. Retail for homeowners and small buyers. Wholesale/supply for contractors, builders, and small industries. Wholesale will grow as reputation builds.

**Reach more contractors or direct home customers:**
Both segments are important. Contractors bring volume and repeat business. Homeowners bring trust-based relationships and retail margin. Goal is to increase contractor base while maintaining homeowner trust.

**Desired reputation in the area:**
"The most reliable electrical and hardware shop — where you get genuine products, fair prices, and honest advice."

## 8. Business Problems (Pain Points)

**Daily problems in running the shop:**
- Managing phone and WhatsApp enquiries while serving walk-in customers
- No systematic way to share product catalog or price list remotely
- Manual inventory tracking leads to occasional stockouts
- Price changes from suppliers hard to track and communicate
- Lost enquiries during busy hours

**Losing customers due to lack of product information:**
Yes. When customers cannot quickly confirm price and availability over phone, they move to the next shop. Information gap directly loses sales.

**Struggle with pricing updates:**
Yes. Supplier prices change frequently. Keeping track manually and updating across all enquiries is time-consuming and error-prone.

**Tasks that take most time every day:**
- Answering repetitive price and availability calls
- Manually checking stock for phone enquiries
- Explaining product differences to customers
- Managing billing and cash

**Most frustrating in customer handling:**
Not being able to attend to all enquiries simultaneously. Losing a customer simply because the phone went unanswered while helping someone in the shop.

A genuine, no-nonsense electrical and hardware business that values customer time and trust. Clean, honest, and professional.

## 10. Success Definition

**How to decide business is growing better:**
- Increase in repeat customer count
- Reduction in lost enquiries (calls answered, enquiries captured)
- More orders coming without needing to explain basics repeatedly
- Steady growth in contractor and wholesale accounts
- Customers mentioning they found the shop online

**More customers, more orders, or more trust?**
Trust first. Trust leads to repeat customers, and repeat customers lead to consistent orders. Customer trust and long-term relationships are the real measure of growth.

**What "success" looks like after 6 months:**
- Website is live and generating steady enquiries
- Customers browse products online before visiting
- Enquiry response time has improved
- Contractor base has grown by 20-30%
- Less time spent on repetitive phone answers

**Problems that should disappear:**
- Lost enquiries due to information unavailability
- Customers not knowing what products are available
- Repeated manual explanation of same products
- Pricing confusion and inconsistency

**If everything works perfectly, daily business changes:**
- Enquiries come through structured channels (form, WhatsApp, phone)
- Customers are pre-informed before visiting
- Less rush-hour chaos, more organized operations
- More time for owner to focus on service quality and business growth
- Staff can handle enquiries with consistent information
